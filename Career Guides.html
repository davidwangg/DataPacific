Introduction

An accessibility tester is a professional who is responsible for evaluating and testing websites, software, and other digital products for accessibility compliance and usability for people with disabilities.

Soft Skills

  • Strong attention to detail
  • Excellent communication skills
  • Ability to work independently
  • Flexibility in working with different types of disabilities

Hard Skills

  • Knowledge of accessibility guidelines and standards, such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)
  • Knowledge of assistive technologies, such as screen readers
  • Ability to use a variety of testing tools, such as accessibility testing software.
  • Knowledge of web development and programming languages

Educational Pathways and Certifications

  • A degree in computer science or related field, or experience in web development, programming, or user experience design, can be beneficial
  • Certifications, such as the Certified Professional in Web Accessibility (CPWA) can also be beneficial

Career Experience

  • Accessibility testing experience in a variety of environments, including e-commerce, government, education, and non-profit organizations
  • Experience working with a variety of assistive technologies, such as screen readers, and knowledge of accessibility guidelines and standards, such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)

Experience Working as a Blind Individual

Some accessibility testers have a personal experience with disability, which can provide valuable insight into the user experience for people with disabilities.

Job Trends in the US

Increasing demand for accessibility testing and compliance due to legal and regulatory requirements, as well as a growing awareness of the need for digital accessibility for people with disabilities.

Key Indicators for a Good Fit

  • Strong attention to detail
  • Excellent communication skills
  • Knowledge of accessibility guidelines and standards, such as the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)
  • Experience working with a variety of assistive technologies, such as screen readers

Introduction

Data analysts use statistical methods to collect, process, and analyze large sets of data to help companies make better business decisions.

Soft Skills

  • Strong communication
  • Strong problem solving skills
  • Strong analytical skills
  • Great attention to detail
  • Ability to work well in a team

Hard Skills

  • Proficiency in statistical analysis
  • Knowledge of programming languages such as R or Python, and databases
  • Knowledge of machine learning techniques is also becoming increasingly important in the field

Educational Pathways and Certifications

  • Bachelor's degree in a related field such as statistics, computer science, or economics
  • Master's degrees in data science or business analytics
  • Many data analysts also pursue certifications such as the Certified Analytics Professional (CAP) or the Microsoft Certified: Azure Data Scientist Associate

Career Experience

A career as a data analyst typically involves working with large sets of data, using statistical methods and software to process and analyze that data, and communicating the results to other members of an organization. This can include tasks such as building predictive models, creating data visualizations, and identifying trends in data.

Experience Working as a Blind Individual

As a blind individual, some of the aspects of data analysis such as data visualization might be tricky and sometimes challenging, however using non-visual methods such as statistical measures to analyze the same data might be useful.

Job Trends in the US

Job trends in the US indicate that demand for data analysts is growing quickly, as more and more companies are recognizing the value of data-driven decision making.

Key Indicators for a Good Fit

  • Strong problem solving and analytical skills
  • Background in a related field such as statistics or computer science

Introduction

A data scientist is a professional who uses statistical and programming skills to extract insights from data. They often work in teams alongside data engineers and business analysts to help organizations make data-driven decisions.

Soft Skills

  • Strong problem-solving abilities
  • Excellent communication skills to effectively present findings to non-technical stakeholders
  • Strong attention to detail
  • Good project management skills
  • Ability to work collaboratively in a team

Hard Skills

  • Programming languages such as Python, R, SQL
  • Experience with data visualization tools (e.g. Tableau, Power BI)
  • Familiarity with machine learning techniques and libraries
  • Knowledge of big data technologies (e.g. Hadoop, Spark)
  • Strong understanding of statistics and statistical modeling

Educational Pathways and Certifications

  • A bachelor's or master's degree in a field such as statistics, computer science, or engineering
  • Some professionals in this field also hold advanced degrees in fields like data science, economics or operation research
  • Certifications from vendors such as Cloudera, Amazon, Microsoft, and IBM

Career Experience

  • Entry-level data scientists are typically hands-on for nearly 1-3 years.
  • As they gain more experience, they may be responsible for leading data science teams, building predictive models, and identifying new business opportunities.
  • They may also specialize in a specific industry or type of data, such as natural language processing or image recognition.

Experience Working as a Blind Individual

  • Data science is a field that relies heavily on visual representation of data
  • However, many data scientists who are blind have developed tools and techniques that allow them to work effectively with data such as developing their own tools or libraries

Job Trends in the US

  • The demand for data scientists is rapidly growing in the US
  • According to Glassdoor, data scientist is ranked as the best job in America for 2021
  • Job growth in this field is expected to continue to increase as more organizations seek to leverage data to drive decision-making

Key Indicators for a Good Fit

  • Strong analytical and mathematical skills
  • A background in programming and computer science
  • Familiarity with machine learning and statistical modeling
  • Strong communication and problem-solving skills
  • Passion for working with data and uncovering insights.

Introduction

Full-stack development is the practice of developing both the front-end and back-end of a website or application. This includes working with both client-side and server-side technologies.

Soft Skills

  • Strong problem-solving skills
  • Good communication skills
  • Adaptability and willingness to learn
  • Strong attention to detail

Hard Skills

  • Proficiency in programming languages such as JavaScript, HTML, and CSS
  • Familiarity with front-end frameworks such as React, Angular, or Vue.js
  • Familiarity with back-end technologies such as Node.js, Python, or Ruby on Rails
  • Knowledge of databases and SQL
  • Experience with version control systems such as Git

Educational Pathways and Certifications

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related field
  • Bootcamp or online course in full-stack development
  • Certifications in specific technologies such as JavaScript, HTML, and CSS

Career Experience

  • Job titles in this field include Full Stack Developer, Full Stack Engineer, and Full Stack Web Developer
  • Responsibilities include designing and developing websites and applications, testing and debugging code, and collaborating with other developers
  • Experience working on both the front-end and back-end of a website or application is essential

Experience Working as a Blind Individual

  • Assistive technologies such as screen readers, keyboard shortcuts, and Braille displays can be used to navigate and interact with code
  • Collaboration with other developers and utilizing other resources such as documentation and tutorials can also be helpful
  • However, front-end can be slightly challenging if you are blind since it involves implementing design and layout details

Job Trends in the US

  • Demand for full-stack developers is increasing as more companies are looking to create and improve their online presence
  • According to Glassdoor, the national average salary for a Full Stack Developer is around $92,000 per year.

Key Indicators for a Good Fit

  • Strong understanding of both front-end and back-end technologies
  • Strong problem-solving skills
  • Ability to work well in a team environment
  • Strong attention to detail
  • Good understanding of development best practices and design patterns
  • Passion for staying up to date with new technologies.

Introduction

IT support professionals provide technical assistance and support related to computer systems, hardware, or software. They are responsible for troubleshooting, diagnosing, and resolving technical issues.

Soft Skills

  • Strong communication skills
  • Good customer service
  • Good problem solving skills
  • Ability to work well under pressure

Hard Skills

  • Knowledge of operating systems, hardware, and software
  • Familiarity with network infrastructure and troubleshooting

Educational Pathways and Certifications

  • A high school diploma or GED is typically the minimum requirement
  • Many employers prefer candidates with an associate or bachelor's degree in computer science or a related field
  • Certifications such as CompTIA A+, CompTIA Network+
  • Microsoft Certified: Windows 10 are also highly valued by employers

Career Experience

Entry-level positions are common, but with experience and additional certifications, IT support professionals can advance to roles such as network administrator, systems administrator, or IT manager.

Experience Working as a Blind Individual

While working as a blind individual in the IT support field can be challenging, advances in technology have made it more accessible. Assistive technologies such as screen readers and keyboard shortcuts can help with tasks such as navigating computer systems and troubleshooting.

Job Trends in the US

  • Demand for IT support professionals is expected to grow as technology continues to play a larger role in businesses and organizations
  • According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of computer support specialists is projected to grow 11% from 2019 to 2029

Key Indicators for a Good Fit

  • Strong problem-solving skills
  • Passion for technology
  • willingness to continue learning and expanding their skills

Introduction

A product manager in the technology industry is responsible for the strategy and development of a product from concept to launch. They work closely with cross-functional teams including engineering, design, marketing, and sales to ensure the product meets the needs of customers and the goals of the company.

Soft Skills

  • Strong communication and leadership skills are essential for a product manager, as they need to be able to effectively communicate with and lead teams of diverse individuals
  • Strategic thinking
  • Good problem solving skills
  • Ability to think creatively

Hard Skills

  • Product managers should have a strong understanding of the technology industry and the specific products or services they are responsible for
  • They should also have experience with product development and project management, as well as knowledge of market research and data analysis

Educational Pathways and Certifications

  • Many product managers have a background in business, marketing, or engineering
  • A Bachelor's degree in one of these fields is often required, but some companies may also consider candidates with relevant work experience
  • Certifications in product management, such as those offered by the Association of International Product Management and Marketing (AIPMM) or the Product Development and Management Association (PDMA), can also be beneficial

Career Experience

A product manager typically begins their career in a junior role and works their way up to a senior or leadership position. They may also have the opportunity to move into related roles such as product marketing or general management.

Experience Working as a Blind Individual

It is possible for blind individuals to work as product managers. A wide range of accessibility tools and technologies are available to support them. They may need to work with other team members to ensure that all materials and information is accessible to them.

Job Trends in the US

  • Demand for product managers is expected to continue growing as the technology industry continues to expand.
  • According to Glassdoor, the average salary for a product manager in the US is around $111,000 per year, and job openings are expected to increase by 7% over the next ten years.

Key Indicators for a Good Fit

An ideal candidate for a product manager role should have

  • Strong communication skills
  • Strong leadership skills
  • A background in business, marketing, or engineering
  • Experience with product development and project management
  • Passion for technology
  • Able to think strategically and creatively

Introduction

A Project Manager in the IT industry is responsible for leading and coordinating the development and implementation of technology-based projects. They work with cross-functional teams to ensure projects are completed on time, within budget, and to the satisfaction of stakeholders.

Soft Skills

  • Strong communication
  • Good leadership skills
  • Good problem solving skills
  • Good time management skills
  • Ability to work well under pressure
  • Ability to handle multiple tasks simultaneously

Hard Skills

  • Familiarity with project management methodologies (such as Agile or Waterfall)
  • Experience using project management software (such as Asana or Jira) is necessary
  • A working knowledge of the specific technology or programming languages being used in the project is beneficial

Educational Pathways and Certifications

  • A bachelor's degree in a related field, such as computer science, information technology, or engineering, is often required
  • certifications such as the Project Management Professional (PMP) or the Certified ScrumMaster (CSM) can be valuable for career advancement

Career Experience

Project management experience in the IT industry is important for success in this role. This can include experience leading software development projects, website design projects, or other technology-based projects.

Experience Working as a Blind Individual

Project managers often have to use presentations and tools that might be inaccessible. This might be a little challenging for blind individuals, however accommodations can help with them being successful in the role. There are several project managers.

Job Trends in the US

  • Demand for IT project managers is expected to grow as the need for technology-based solutions continues to increase.
  • According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of computer and information technology occupations is projected to grow 11% from 2019 to 2029, much faster than the average for all occupations.

Key Indicators for a Good Fit

A good fit for the role of a Project Manager in the IT industry would be

  • Someone who is able to lead a team
  • Someone who is able to manage multiple projects
  • Someone who is able to communicate effectively
  • An individual with a strong understanding of the technology or programming languages used in their projects
  • Able to work well under pressure

Introduction

A QA tester is a professional responsible for testing software and identifying any bugs or issues before the product is released to the public. They work in the software development process to ensure the quality of the final product.

Soft Skills

  • Strong attention to detail
  • Good communication skills
  • Ability to work well in a team
  • Strong problem-solving skills

Hard Skills

  • Knowledge of software testing methodologies and tools
  • Understanding of programming languages and databases
  • Experience with bug tracking software
  • Familiarity with different operating systems and devices

Educational Pathways and Certifications

  • A degree in computer science or a related field is often preferred, but not always required.
  • Some organizations may require certifications in specific software testing tools or methodologies.

Career Experience

  • QA testers typically start as entry-level testers and work their way up to senior or lead roles.
  • Experience working in different industries or on different types of software can be beneficial.
  • Some QA testers may also work as freelance or contract testers

Experience Working as a Blind Individual

The specific platforms being used might be inaccessible, but accommodations can help be successful.

Job Trends in the US

  • The demand for QA testers is expected to grow in the coming years, as the software industry continues to expand.
  • Some job opportunities may be affected by the increasing use of automation in the testing process.

Key Indicators for a Good Fit

  • Strong attention to detail and a passion for finding and fixing bugs
  • Good problem-solving and analytical skills
  • Ability to work well under pressure and meet deadlines
  • Strong interest in technology and software development.

Introduction

Software engineering is the process of designing, developing, testing, and maintaining software. It involves the application of engineering principles to the creation of software.

Soft Skills

  • Strong problem solving skills
  • Good communication skills
  • Ability to work well in a team
  • Adaptability

Hard Skills

  • Programming languages (such as Java, Python, C++)
  • Software development methodologies (such as Agile, Scrum)
  • Software testing and debugging
  • Version control (such as Git)

Educational Pathways and Certifications

  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, or a related field
  • Master's degree in above fields
  • Professional certifications such as Certified Software Development Professional (CSDP) or Microsoft Certified: Azure Developer Associate
  • Several software engineering bootcamps are also available. Some of the more popular ones include App Academy, General Assembly, Codesmith, DevCodeCamp, Flatiron school among others. You may want to check on the accessibility of these bootcamps before enrolling.

Career Experience

  • As a software engineer, you may work in a variety of industries such as technology, finance, healthcare, and more.
  • Job responsibilities may include designing and developing software, testing and debugging code, and collaborating with other team members.
  • Entry-level roles may include software developer or software engineer, while more experienced roles may include senior software engineer or lead software engineer.
  • One may also start working out for smaller companies such as for service-based companies, and then transition to product-based companies.
  • Starting out as an intern or apprentice is also fairly common.

Experience Working as a Blind Individual

  • Blind individuals may face additional challenges in the field of software engineering, such as difficulty with visual debugging tools and limited accessibility of software development environments.
  • However, often accommodations are available to help with these challenges such as on-demand visual assistance. One can also use their coding skills to develop scripts to make applications more compatible and accessible with their assistive technologies such as screen readers.

Job Trends in the US

  • The demand for software engineers is expected to grow in the coming years, driven by the increasing use of technology in various industries.
  • According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment of software developers is projected to grow 21% from 2019 to 2029, much faster than the average for all occupations.

Key Indicators for a Good Fit

  • Strong problem-solving and analytical skills
  • Strong attention to detail
  • Ability to work well in a team environment
  • Team player

Introduction

Tech sales is a profession that involves selling technology products and services to businesses and organizations.

Soft Skills

  • Strong negotiation skills
  • Strong communication skills
  • Ability to build and maintain relationships with clients

Hard Skills

  • Knowledge of the specific technology products and services being sold
  • Understanding of the broader technology landscape and trends.

Educational Pathways and Certifications

  • Degree in a technology-related field, such as computer science or engineering, as well as business or sales-related degrees
  • Certifications in specific technology products or services can also be beneficial

Career Experience

Career experience in tech sales can include roles such as account executive, sales engineer, and sales manager, with opportunities for advancement to leadership positions.

Experience Working as a Blind Individual

Experience working as a blind individual in tech sales may involve the use of assistive technology and accommodations to assist with tasks such as reading and writing emails and documents.

Job Trends in the US

Job trends in the US show increasing demand for tech sales professionals as technology becomes increasingly integrated into all aspects of business and society.

Key Indicators for a Good Fit

  • A strong understanding of technology
  • The ability to effectively communicate and negotiate
  • The ability to build and maintain relationships with clients
